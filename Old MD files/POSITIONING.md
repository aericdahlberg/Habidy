## The Problem We're Solving

From user research with students, six clear pain points emerged:

**Pain Point 1 — Goals feel unclear and hard to start**
"I don't know what I want." Vague ideas of who they want to become, without structure
to explore. Goals are often triggered by external pressure (new semesters, deadlines,
FOMO), not identity. Students lack the knowledge to align tasks to goals or break them
into manageable pieces.

**Pain Point 2 — Can't break goals into habits**
Missing specialized knowledge and foresight to accurately break down and align tasks/habits
to their goals. Goal-setting feels scary and tedious, not energizing.
Example: A student who wants to be a software engineer but doesn't enjoy coding for
2 hours a week. The question to ask: do you actually want this, or is it external pressure?
Search for habits you LOVE to do — that's what should inform identity.

**Pain Point 3 — Implementation breaks down**
Habits feel unpersonalized — hard to see how tasks connect to personal goals and lifestyle.
Students lack psychological frameworks (cue/craving/action/reward) to implement habits well.
Example: Starting with "apply to jobs 2 hours every day." When strong motivation: fine.
Quickly becomes tedious and frightening. The habit drops.
Often it's not about what you do during a habit — it's about starting one.

**Pain Point 4 — Tracking feels like work**
"Tracking feels like work." Reminders become annoying. Manual logging is tedious
and easy to abandon. Tedious to track EVERY habit.

**Pain Point 5 — Life disrupts streaks**
When life gets busy or motivation drops, users want habits that adapt to energy and schedule
so they can stay consistent without burning out.

**Pain Point 6 — No visible progress**
Users can't see what's working. No connection between daily actions and long-term goals.
No clear reflection or pattern recognition.

## The Habit Cycle Students Know Too Well

```
Set Goal → Feeling motivated → Build Schedule → Translate to daily actions
    → Initial consistency → Early wins feel great
        → Friction appears → Life gets in the way
            → Breakdown → Motivation fades, streak breaks
                → Reset → Goal fades, start over
```

This cycle creates a sense of chronic underachievement —
goals feel possible only in a future with a "better" schedule, never today.

## Target User

**Primary:** College students, initially Boston University
**Secondary:** All university students in the US
**Psychographic:** Goal-driven students who feel stuck between who they are and who
they want to be. Not necessarily high achievers — students who *want* to do more
but don't know how to make it stick.

---

## User Stories (Jobs To Be Done)

1. *When I'm figuring out who I want to be, I want to explore my identity and goals,
   so I can understand what matters to me and build habits that actually reflect that.*

2. *When I have a vague goal, I want help breaking it down into specific habits,
   so I can take action without feeling overwhelmed.*

3. *When I'm implementing a new habit, I want it to fit naturally into my schedule
   and feel connected to my goals, so I can see the point of doing it every day.*

4. *When life gets busy or my motivation drops, I want habits that adapt to my energy
   and schedule, so I can stay consistent without burning out.*

5. *When I accomplish or miss a habit, I want a system to track them with minimal
   to no effort.*

6. *When I've been completing habits over time, I want to see my progress and understand
   what's working and what isn't, so I can double down on what moves me toward my goals.*

## Revenue Model

**Free Tier**
Daily habits + limited AI coaching. Low barrier to entry drives adoption and
word-of-mouth growth on campus.

**Premium — $5–$10/month**
Advanced AI coaching, premium insights, app integrations (Google Calendar, Notion),
and long-term analytics for serious habit builders.

## Market Size

**BU Launch (Conservative)**
- TAM: 36,000 BU undergrad + graduate students
- SAM: 6,500 arriving students open to new resources
- SOM: 150 early adopters → 1,500 after word-of-mouth
- Early revenue: $5 × 15 paying students = $75/month (validates willingness to pay)

**All Universities**
- TAM: 18.6M total US university students
- SAM: 3.35M arriving university students
- SOM: 670,000 goal-driven students (20%)
- Revenue at 1% premium: $33,500/month

## Go-To-Market (BU First)
